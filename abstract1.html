Provably Safe Robotics for Autonomous Science: Real-Time Formal Verification with the Verified Agentic Pipeline (VAP)

Saien Deng, David Scott Lewis, Enrique Zueco, 1st Workshop on Embodied AI and Robotics for Future Scientific Discovery at International Conference on Intelligent Robots and Systems (IROS), 2025

pdf · poster

Deploying embodied AI for autonomous science requires provable safety guarantees, yet formal verification is often dismissed as computationally prohibitive for real-time robotic control. This paper empirically refutes that assumption. We introduce the Verified Agentic Pipeline (VAP), an architecture that integrates physics‑informed machine learning, high‑fidelity simulation, and formal verification gates to provide pre‑execution safety guarantees for embodied scientific agents. We rigorously validate VAP using Hertzian contact mechanics as a representative physics domain, demonstrating that our verification approach achieves a mean latency of 0.323 ms (SD=0.089 ms, 99th percentile=0.621 ms)—well within the computational budget of modern robotic control loops (typically 1–10 ms). This result establishes that provable safety is not only theoretically sound but practically viable for real‑time autonomous systems in scientific laboratories. By demonstrating the computational feasibility of real‑time formal verification, this work opens a pathway toward trustworthy Robot Scientists capable of high‑stakes, unsupervised experimental workflows.
