Gemini-ER 1.5 × Physics-Informed Neural Operators: Toward Robots that Think in Language and React in Physics at Low Latency

Saien Deng, David Scott Lewis, Enrique Zueco

pdf

We propose a hierarchical architecture that couples the Gemini Robotics-ER 1.5 large-multimodal model with Physics-Informed Neural Operators (PINO) to build robots that think in language and react in physics at low latency. Gemini Robotics-ER 1.5, accessed through Google AI Studio, generates JSON skill graphs, edge-case simulation parameters, and prototype Python controllers in ∼2 s cloud calls. A two-layer PINO embeds rigid-body and complementarity constraints to forecast contact-rich dynamics in 0.8 ms and supplies calibrated uncertainty that a 1 kHz MPC-CBF loop uses for safety filtering. In Brax simulations of press-fit and peg-in-slot tasks, the PINO reduces 5-step prediction error by 42% and maintains stability under disturbances better than black-box baselines, all while meeting sub-millisecond replanning budgets. Ablations that remove physics penalties or safety verification reveal 3–5× higher collision rates. We release prompts, cached Gemini outputs, PINO training code, and a Gemma 3 fallback to foster reproducible, robust, real-world robot learning.
